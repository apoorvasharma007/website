Chest

chest push hypertrophy

Anatomy

The pectoralis major has two heads: the clavicular (upper) and the sternal (lower/mid). Most pressing movements work both, but incline angle shifts emphasis toward the upper head.

Primary exercises

  • Flat barbell bench press — high load, bilateral, good for progressive overload
  • Incline dumbbell press — upper chest emphasis, greater stretch at the bottom
  • Cable fly (low-to-high) — constant tension, good finisher

Key cues

  • Retract and depress the scapula before unracking
  • Drive feet into the floor, maintain arch
  • Lower the bar to the lower chest, not the clavicle
  • Think “bend the bar” to engage lats and protect the shoulder

Programming notes

2–4 working sets, 6–15 rep range depending on goal. Chest responds well to volume; prioritise stretch-focused exercises for hypertrophy.

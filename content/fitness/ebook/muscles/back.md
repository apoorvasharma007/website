## Anatomy

The back is a large, layered group: latissimus dorsi (width), traps (upper/mid/lower), rhomboids, and spinal erectors. Most people under-train the lower traps and erectors.

## Primary exercises

- **Barbell row** — high load horizontal pull, good for thickness
- **Pull-up / lat pulldown** — vertical pull, lat width
- **Cable row (seated)** — constant tension, good mind-muscle connection
- **Face pull** — rear delts and upper traps, often neglected

## Key cues

- Initiate pulls by depressing the scapula, not by curling the wrist
- On rows: lead with the elbow, not the hand
- Full stretch at the top of pulldowns/pull-ups

## Programming notes

Back tolerates high volume well. Include both vertical and horizontal pulls each week. Grip fatigue often limits performance — use straps when needed to train the target muscle rather than grip.
